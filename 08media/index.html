MLB history

Baseball Orginates

Baseball's origins can be traced back to various bat-and-ball games played in England, such as stoolball and rounders. These games were brought to North America by English colonists in the 18th century and shared similarities with the modern game of baseball.

In the early 19th century, variations of these games were played on both sides of the Atlantic under names like "town ball", "round ball", and "base-ball". Modern baseball rules were significantly shaped by Wikipediaamateur clubs in New York City in the mid-19th century nicknamed the "New York style".

MLB Hisory and how it became to what we know today

Major League Baseball (MLB) has a rich history stemming from the rise of professional baseball in the late 19th century in the United States. It is considered the oldest major professional sports league in the world. MLB is currently composed of 30 teams, divided equally between the National League (NL) and the American League (AL).

  • 1869: The first all-professional team, the Cincinnati Red Stockings, was founded.
  • 1876: The National League was established as the first major league.
  • 1901: The American League declared itself a major league, leading to a "baseball war" with the NL.
  • 1903: The National Agreement was signed, formalizing the relationship between the AL and NL and establishing the World Series
  • 1900-1919: Known as the "dead-ball era," characterized by low-scoring games and pitcher dominance.
  • 1919: The Black Sox Scandal rocked the sport when eight Chicago White Sox players conspired to throw the World Series.
  • 1920: The office of the Commissioner of Baseball was created to govern the sport.
  • 1947: Jackie Robinson broke baseball's color barrier, debuting with the Brooklyn Dodgers.
  • 1961: The first expansion era began with the addition of new teams and the season extended to 162 games.
  • 1973: The American League adopted the designated hitter rule.
  • 1975: The reserve clause was abolished, leading to the introduction of free agency for players.
  • 2000: The NL and AL legally merged into a single organization, Major League Baseball.
  • 2020: MLB formally recognized seven Negro Leagues from 1920-1948 as major leagues.
  • 2024: MLB announced the incorporation of Negro League statistics into its official historical record.
  • 2023: A pitch clock was introduced to speed up the pace of play.
