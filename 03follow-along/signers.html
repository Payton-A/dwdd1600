Who were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declartion of Independence

John Hancock
Signed the Declaration of Independence as a delegate from Massachusetts.
His large, elaborate signature is the most prominent on the document.
Served as president of the Second Continental Congress at the time of the signing.
Benjamin Franklin
A delegate from Pennsylvania and the oldest signer of the Declaration at age 70.
He was a renowned inventor, scientist, diplomat, and statesman.
Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration of Independence, representing Virginia.
Later became the third President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
A delegate from Massachusetts and a strong opponent of British taxation.
Known as a firebrand of the American Revolution.
George Clymer
A delegate from Pennsylvania who also signed the U.S. Constitution.
He was a merchant and helped fund the Revolution.
Roger Sherman
A delegate from Connecticut who helped draft the Declaration and also signed the Constitution.
He was the only person to sign all four great state papers of the United States: the Continental Association, the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the Constitution.
Button Gwinnett
A delegate from Georgia, he was one of the signers born in the British Isles.
His signature is one of the most sought-after by collectors.
John Adams
A delegate from Massachusetts who was a leading advocate for independence.
Later became the second President of the United States.

The signers were not just delegates; many were leaders in their colonies and later played vital roles in the formation of the U.S. government, with several signing the Constitution as well. The act of signing was a dangerous one, as the signers risked being charged with treason by the British Crown.
